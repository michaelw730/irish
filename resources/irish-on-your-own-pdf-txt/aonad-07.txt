There are different ways of saying ‘to’ in Irish, depending on where 
you’re going. Go is used in front of names of places and other nouns 
that are not preceded by the article an (the): 

Go Baile Átha Cliath 
Go hArd Mhacha* 
Go Dún Pádraig 

To Dublin 
To Armagh 
To Downpatrick 

*Go doesn’t affect words that begin with a consonant, but if the name 
begins with a vowel, h is placed before it. 

Go dtí is used before nouns that are preceded by the article: 

Tá mé ag dul go dtí an t-ospidéal. 

I’m going to the hospital. 

The word chuig is used instead of go or go dtí when you’re talking 
about going to an event or to see a person. We’ll deal with chuig in 
more detail in Aonad 11.
