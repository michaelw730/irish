ASKING SOMEONE HOW THEY FEEL 

Cad é mar a bhraitheann tú? 

How do you feel? 

The phrase Cad é mar atá tú? (How are you?) is generally used as a greeting, but it can also 
be used to find out how someone is feeling. 

You learnt in Aonad 14 how to ask what’s wrong with someone: 

Cad é atá ort? 

Cad é atá air? 

Cad é atá uirthi? 

What’s wrong with you? 

What’s wrong with him? 

What’s wrong with her? 

SAYING HOW YOU FEEL 

Tá mé go breá.

Ní bhraithim go maith. 

Tá mé tinn. 

I’m fine. 

I’m only middling. 

I don’t feel well. 

I’m ill.

NAMING ILLNESSES 

Tá tinneas cinn orm. 

Tá droim nimhneach air. 

I have a headache. 

He has a sore back. 

Tá bolg nimhneach uirthi. 

She has a stomach-ache. 

or 

Tá pain ina bolg. 

Tá slaghdán orthu. 

Tá droch-chasachtach orm. 

She has a pain in her stomach. 

They have a cold. 

I have a bad cough. 

Tá sceadamán nimhneach uirthi. 

She has a sore throat. 

Listen to some people saying what’s wrong with them. 

–An bhfuil Seosamh tinn? 
–Tá. Tá sceadamán nimhneach air. 

–Cad é atá ort? 
–Bolg nimhneach. 

–Cad é mar a bhraitheann tú? 
–Ní bhraithim go maith. Tá tinneas cinn orm, agus tá pian i mo bholg.

CANCELLING ARRANGEMENTS 

Sometimes it’s necessary to cancel arrangements because of illness or some injury. Here are 
a few useful phrases that can be used in such a situation. 

Aonad 22 

Ní bheidh mé ábalta a dhul amach 

I won’t be able to go out tonight. 

anocht. 

Ní bheidh mé ag an chruinniú anocht. 

I won’t be at the meeting tonight. 

If you’re asked the question: 

An mbeidh tú ansin anocht? 

Will you be there tonight? 

you can reply: 

Beidh. 
or 
Ní bheidh. 

I will. 

I won’t. 

If you feel it necessary to apologise you can use the following: 

Tá brón orm. 

I’m sorry.

SAYING YOU’RE BETTER OR WORSE 

Tá mé ag teacht chugam féin. 

I’m recovering. 

Tá me níos fearr. 

I’m better. 

Níl mé níos fearr–tá mé níos measa. 

I’m not better–I’m worse. 

Aonad 22 

The word níos is used when comparing things 
in Irish. Words that follow níos are generally 
changed: 

deas 
níos deise 

fuar 
níos fuaire 

nice 
nicer 

cold 
colder 

Adjectives that are irregular change completely: 

maith 
níos fearr 

olc 
níos measa 

good 
better 

bad 
worse
