AG MALARTÚ SCÉALTA NUACHTA  EXCHANGING NEWS 

ASKING SOMEONE IF THEY HAVE ANY NEWS 

An bhfuil nuacht ar bith agat? 

Have you any news? 

Here is a common response: 

Muise, níl. 

Indeed, I haven’t. 

ASKING SOMEONE IF THERE’S ANYTHING HAPPENING 

An bhfuil a dhath ag dul? 

Is there anything happening? 

Níl mórán. 

Not much. 

TELLING GOOD NEWS 

Fuair m’fhear céile post úr. 

My husband got a new job. 

Cheannaigh mé carr úr Dé Luain. 

I bought a new car on Monday. 

Bhí leanbh ag Bríd arú aréir. 

Bríd had a baby the night before last. 

REACTING TO GOOD NEWS 

Sin dea-scéala! 

That’s good news! 

Tá lúcháir orm sin a chloisteáil. 

I’m glad to hear that. 

Is maith sin. 

Comhghairdeachas! 

That’s good. 

Congratulations! 

Listen to some people reacting to good news. Look out for go fóill (yet). 

–An bhfuair Maria post úr go fóill? 

–Fuair. Tá sí ag obair i monarcha i mBéal Feirste. 

–Is maith sin. 

–An raibh an leanbh ag do bhean chéile go fóill? 

–Bhí. Bhí cailín beag aici arú aréir. 

–Sin dea-scéala. Comhghairdeachas!

QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS IN THE PAST TENSE 
We already looked at how the past tense is formed (Aonad 11). We’ll now 
turn our attention to asking and answering in that tense. 

REGULAR VERBS 

With regular verbs, you place Ar before the past tense form: 

Cheannaigh mé .... 

Ar cheannaigh tú ...? 

I bought .. 

Did you buy...? 

Cheannaigh. / Níor cheannaigh. 

I did (buy). / I didn’t (buy). 

Ar bhuail tú le Laura? 

Bhuail. / Níor bhuail. 

IRREGULAR VERBS 

Did you meet Laura? 

I did (meet). / I didn’t (meet). 

These are commonly used verbs, so it’s worth learning the correct forms. 

An raibh tú ...? 

Bhí. / Ní raibh. 

An ndearna tú ...? 

Rinne. / Ní dhearna. 

Ar chuala tú ...? 

Chuala. / Níor chuala. 

An bhfuair tú ...? 

Fuair. / Ní bhfuair. 

Ar thug tú ...? 

Thug. / Níor thug. 

An ndeachaigh tú ...? 

Chuaigh. / Ní dheachaigh 

Ar tháinig tú ...? 

Tháinig. / Níor tháinig 

Were you ...? 

Did you do / make ...? 

Did you hear ...? 

Did you get ...? 

Did you give ...? 

Did you go ...? 

Did you come ...? 

An is usually not pronounced if it precedes the verb.  We’ll be returning to 
the past tense in Aonad 29.

Listen to people reacting to bad news. You’ll hear this new word: timpiste 
(accident). 

–Ar chuala tú faoi Shéamas? 
–Níor chuala. 
–Bhí timpiste aige maidin inné. Thit sé sa bhaile agus bhris sé a chos.
